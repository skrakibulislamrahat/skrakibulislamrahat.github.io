About

My work lies at the intersection of machine learning and healthcare. I focus on identifying failure modes in medical AI systems, including dataset bias, shortcut learning, and poor calibration, and on designing evaluation frameworks that expose and mitigate these issues.

Research Projects

Exposing Dataset Artifacts in Medical AI

An IJBHI-level empirical study demonstrating how diabetic retinopathy models exploit non-pathological artifacts such as image borders and acquisition cues, leading to inflated internal performance and degraded external generalization.

GitHub Repository

Lightweight Diabetic Retinopathy Detection Models

Comparative evaluation of MobileNetV2, EfficientNet-B0, and SqueezeNet for DR screening, emphasizing accuracy–efficiency trade-offs for mobile and low-resource clinical deployment.

GitHub Repository

Multimodal Framework Research

Development of multimodal learning pipelines integrating medical images and structured clinical data to study robustness, interpretability, and generalization in disease prediction tasks.
