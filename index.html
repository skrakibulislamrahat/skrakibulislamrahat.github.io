About

My research lies at the intersection of machine learning and healthcare, where I develop and evaluate AI systems for medical imaging and clinical decision support. I am particularly interested in understanding and mitigating failure modes in medical AI, including shortcut learning, poor calibration, and performance degradation under external validation.

Research Interests

  • Medical Image Analysis (Fundus, Mammography, SPECT)
  • Multimodal Learning (Imaging + Clinical/EHR Data)
  • Explainable AI (Grad-CAM, SHAP)
  • Model Calibration and Reliability
  • Dataset Bias and Robust Evaluation

Research Projects

  • Artifact Bias in Fundus Image Models
    Identified shortcut learning caused by image borders and acquisition artifacts; evaluated robustness using external datasets and Grad-CAM analysis.
  • Calibration-Aware Diabetic Retinopathy Screening
    Studied probabilistic reliability, ECE, Brier score, and temperature scaling across internal and external validation cohorts.
  • Multimodal Breast Cancer Risk Prediction
    Integrated mammography and clinical EHR features for early detection using deep learning.
