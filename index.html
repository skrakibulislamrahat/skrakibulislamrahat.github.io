About

My research lies at the intersection of machine learning and healthcare. I study how medical AI systems fail in real-world settings due to dataset artifacts, shortcut learning, and poor calibration, and I develop evaluation frameworks that expose and mitigate these issues.

Research Projects

Exposing Dataset Artifacts in Medical AI (IJBHI-level Study)
Investigated shortcut learning in diabetic retinopathy models trained on fundus images. Demonstrated that models exploit non-pathological artifacts such as borders and acquisition cues, leading to inflated internal performance and degraded external generalization.
GitHub Repository
Lightweight DR Detection Models
Systematic evaluation of MobileNetV2, EfficientNetB0, and SqueezeNet for diabetic retinopathy screening, emphasizing accuracy–efficiency trade-offs for mobile and low-resource clinical settings.
GitHub Repository
Multimodal Framework Research
Development of multimodal learning pipelines integrating medical images and structured clinical data to study performance, robustness, and interpretability in disease prediction tasks.
GitHub Repository
