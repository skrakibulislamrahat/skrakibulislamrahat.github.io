About

My work lies at the intersection of machine learning and healthcare, with an emphasis on understanding how medical image classifiers behave under realistic evaluation constraints. I focus on identifying failure modes related to miscalibration, dataset artifacts, and distribution shift, and on reporting results using protocol-faithful, reproducible evaluation pipelines.

Research Projects

Calibration Analysis Under Counterfactual Consistency Regularization for Referable Diabetic Retinopathy

An IEEE J-BHI–style methodological evaluation of probabilistic calibration in a ResNet-18 fundus classifier trained on APTOS 2019. The study reports multi-seed internal discrimination and calibration metrics (ECE15, Brier score, reliability diagrams), applies post-hoc temperature scaling on internal data only, and provides a constrained supporting external diagnostic evaluation on Messidor-2 under a strict no-adaptation protocol.

The work explicitly separates internal calibration analysis from external behavior under dataset shift and makes no claims of deployment readiness, aligning with conservative evaluation standards in medical imaging.

Lightweight Diabetic Retinopathy Detection Models

Comparative evaluation of MobileNetV2, EfficientNet-B0, and SqueezeNet for diabetic retinopathy screening, focusing on accuracy–efficiency trade-offs relevant to low-resource and mobile clinical settings.
